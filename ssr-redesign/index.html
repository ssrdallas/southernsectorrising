Our Story

Born from Injustice. Built for Justice.

Southern Sector Rising emerged in 2019 when a mountain of toxic shingles was illegally dumped next to a Black neighborhood in South Dallas. We organized. We fought. We won.

Today, we center the perspectives of frontline communities combating environmental injustices across Dallas and beyond. We operate according to the Jemez Principles of Democratic Organizing Justice, ensuring that those most affected lead the fight.

Our work has expanded from one neighborhood to regional, national, and international coalitions—because environmental racism knows no borders, and neither does our commitment to justice.

Our Work

Five Pillars of Justice

Our integrated approach addresses the interconnected challenges facing frontline communities.

Environmental Justice

Fighting racist zoning, illegal dumping, and industrial pollution that disproportionately impacts BIPOC communities.

  • Shingle Mountain removal
  • Environmental Justice Tours
  • Policy advocacy

Climate Resilience

Building community capacity to adapt and thrive in the face of climate change impacts.

  • International climate delegations
  • Resilience planning
  • Community education

Food Sovereignty

Ensuring communities have control over their food systems and access to healthy, culturally appropriate food.

  • Community gardens
  • Food access programs
  • Urban agriculture

Labor & Economic Justice

Advocating for fair wages, safe working conditions, and economic opportunity for all workers.

  • Worker advocacy
  • Economic development
  • Coalition building

Community HUBs

Creating spaces for organizing, education, mutual aid, and community connection.

  • Resource centers
  • Mutual aid networks
  • Community organizing

Our Impact

Measurable Change

Shingle Mountain Victory

After three years of organizing, legal action, and relentless advocacy, the 200,000+ tons of illegally dumped shingles were removed from our community.

Clean Water Access

Ongoing water deliveries to Sandbranch ensure residents have access to safe drinking water while we fight for infrastructure investment.

Community Education

Environmental Justice Tours have educated hundreds about the history and ongoing reality of environmental racism in Dallas.

Coalition Building

Built a network of 150+ allied organizations across environmental justice, climate, labor, and food sovereignty movements.
